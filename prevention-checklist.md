# 🔐 Stay Safe Online — Checklist (For College Students in India)

## 1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords  
- Create passwords with at least 12 characters (mix of letters, numbers, symbols).  
- Never reuse the same password across apps (especially email + banking).

## 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)  
- Turn on 2FA for email, social media, and banking apps.  
- Prefer authenticator apps over SMS when possible.

## 3. Beware of Phishing Links & Messages  
- Don’t click on suspicious links from unknown emails, WhatsApp, or SMS.  
- Always verify URLs before entering login details.

## 4. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks  
- Don’t access banking, UPI apps, or personal accounts on public Wi-Fi.  
- If necessary, use a trusted VPN.

## 5. Keep Your Devices Updated  
- Regularly update your phone, laptop, and apps.  
- Install apps only from official stores like Google Play or App Store.

---

## 💸 UPI & Financial Safety (Important)

## 6. Never Share OTP, PIN, or Approve Unknown Requests  
- No bank or app will ever ask for your OTP or UPI PIN.  
- Reject unknown “Collect Request” payments in apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.

## 7. Double-Check Before Sending Money  
- Verify the recipient’s name and UPI ID before confirming payment.  
- Even small mistakes can send money to the wrong person instantly.

## 8. Avoid Screen Sharing During Financial Transactions  
- Scammers may ask you to share your screen via apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer.  
- Never allow this when using banking or UPI apps.

---

## 📱 Social & Personal Safety

## 9. Limit What You Share Online  
- Avoid posting personal details like address, college ID, or phone number publicly.  
- Set your social profiles to private when possible.

## 10. Log Out from Shared Devices  
- Always log out from cyber cafés or shared laptops.  
- Don’t save passwords on public systems.

## 11. Install Only Trusted Apps & Extensions  
- Avoid downloading cracked software or unknown APKs.  
- Check reviews and permissions before installing apps.

---

## 🚨 If Something Goes Wrong

- Report immediately on the **National Cyber Crime Portal**: https://cybercrime.gov.in  
- Call the cybercrime helpline: **1930** (for quick action on financial fraud)
